Lake Norman service guide

Boat detailing services.

Compare routine cleaning, interior restoration, gelcoat correction, marine protection, pontoon and bottom care, and seasonal service before requesting a quote.

✦

Service catalog

Cleaning, correction, and protection are different jobs.

The final scope should identify what will be cleaned, what defects will be corrected, what protection will be applied, and what results cannot be guaranteed.

01
◌

Full mobile boat detail

A comprehensive interior-and-exterior reset for a maintained or moderately dirty boat.

Common scope

  • Exterior rinse, marine-safe wash, and dry
  • Glass, trim, rails, cupholders, tables, and accessible compartments
  • Interior vacuuming and surface cleaning
  • Vinyl cleaning and appropriate conditioning
  • Carpet, woven flooring, or deck cleaning
  • Light water-spot and surface-contaminant treatment
Quote depends on: boat length, layout, amount of storage, condition, utilities, and work access.
02
◇

Interior deep clean, mold & mildew care

Targeted interior service for boats stored under covers, exposed to humidity, or affected by food, sunscreen, spills, pets, and moisture.

Common scope

  • Seats, seams, crevices, bolsters, and under-seat areas
  • Cubbies, cupholders, tables, lockers, and compartments
  • Carpet extraction or floor scrubbing where appropriate
  • Stain, mold, and mildew treatment based on material
  • Odor-reduction and drying recommendations
  • UV-compatible protectant when selected
Important: permanent dye transfer, cracked vinyl, deep mold penetration, and material failure may not be fully reversible.
03
✦

Gelcoat oxidation & gloss correction

Multi-stage defect removal for chalkiness, haze, fading, water spots, light scratches, and lost gloss.

Possible stages

  • Wash, decontamination, and surface inspection
  • Test spot to establish a realistic outcome
  • Water-spot or mineral-deposit treatment
  • Heavy, medium, or finishing compound
  • Machine polishing and gloss refinement
  • Wet sanding only when condition and material allow
Restoration limit: severe gelcoat loss, deep scratches, repairs, repainting, or failed surfaces require specialist evaluation.
04
⬡

Marine sealant or ceramic protection

Protection selected after the surface is cleaned and corrected to the required level.

Options may include

  • Shorter-term marine wax or sealant
  • Synthetic protection for maintained gelcoat
  • Multi-step ceramic coating systems
  • Separate treatment for glass, metal, vinyl, or nonskid
  • Maintenance instructions and follow-up washes
  • Documented preparation before coating
Ask the provider: product, preparation stages, claimed durability, maintenance, exclusions, and warranty terms.
05
≈

Pontoon tubes, waterline & bottom cleaning

Specialty cleaning for algae, scum, waterline staining, and buildup below the normal wash area.

Common requests

  • Pontoon and tritoon tube cleaning or brightening
  • Fiberglass hull-bottom and waterline cleaning
  • Algae and seasonal buildup removal
  • Acid-based treatment by a qualified provider when appropriate
  • Lift, forklift, trailer, or hauled-out access
  • Pickup or marina coordination when offered
Access rule: this work should only be quoted after confirming lift access, safe runoff handling, facility permission, and provider qualifications.
06
↗

Seasonal, maintenance & pre-sale service

Scheduled care built around Lake Norman’s boating season and the owner’s use pattern.

Common plans

  • Spring opening detail before peak season
  • Recurring wash or interior maintenance
  • Mid-season water-spot and mildew control
  • Fall cleanup and storage preparation
  • Pre-sale cosmetic preparation and photo readiness
  • Post-purchase reset for a used boat
Best practice: define frequency, included surfaces, response expectations, and what triggers an additional restoration quote.

Vessels commonly requested

Quote by layout and surface—not only by length.

01

Pontoons & tritoons

Large interiors, under-seat storage, vinyl, woven floors or carpet, exterior panels, and aluminum tubes.

02

Wake, ski & surf boats

Detailed interiors, towers, ballast-area access, dark gelcoat, water spots, and heavy lake-season use.

03

Bowriders & runabouts

Interior and exterior details, gelcoat polishing, upholstery care, compartments, and pre-sale preparation.

04

Cruisers & cabin boats

Larger surface area, cabin and enclosure materials, multiple compartments, longer timelines, and access planning.

05

Fishing boats

Nonskid decks, livewell and storage areas, fish residue, aluminum or fiberglass surfaces, and trailer access.

06

Jet skis & personal watercraft

Wash, water-spot removal, plastic and trim care, seat cleaning, gloss improvement, and protection.
